# Task Scheduling and Transmission for Distributed Job Execution

Deploying data centers across geographically distributed locations has become common practice for exploiting data locality to meet strict latency and reliability requirements of various services. This paper studies a job scheduling problem for distributed job execution, in which the data partitions required by the tasks of jobs can be transferred between different sites leveraging network resources in the system. Our goal is to minimize the average job response time by jointly optimizing job scheduling and task transmission. We present a Workload-Aware Selection with Transmission algorithm, which coordinates job scheduling across different sites with task transmission and aims to provide a near-optimal solution. Our approach starts with a task transmission strategy tailored to optimize the response time of a single job. We then develop an integrated solution that jointly schedules multiple jobs along with their associated task transmissions. To balance performance and computational efficiency, we also present a fast heuristic approximation algorithm. Experiments with real job traces from Alibaba production clusters demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed algorithms under various system utilization and bandwidth settings.
